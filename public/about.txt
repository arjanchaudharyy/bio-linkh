### 3. Youngest Researcher Assigned a CVE
Received a CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures) identifier, making him one of the youngest researchers globally to achieve this distinction.

**What is CVE:**
CVE is an internationally recognized reference system for publicly known cybersecurity vulnerabilities. Receiving a CVE means discovering a significant, verified vulnerability affecting widely-used software or systems.

**Significance:**
- CVE assignments are highly prestigious in the cybersecurity community
- Places researcher in permanent public record of security contributors
- Demonstrates discovery of significant vulnerability with real-world impact
- Youngest or among the youngest people ever to receive this designation
